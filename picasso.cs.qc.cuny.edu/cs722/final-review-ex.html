Space Complexity and Its Relation to Time Complexity

5. Give a precise definition of worst-case space complexity functions of DTMs and NTMs that halt on all input strings.

6. Let M be any DTM or NTM that halts on all input strings. Prove the following:
  1. WMspace(n) ≤ WMtime(n) + 1.
  2. WMtime(n) ≤ (|Γ|+|Q|)WMspace(n)+1, where Γ is the tape alphabet of M and Q is the state set of M. Hint: Use the possible configurations of M.
7. Prove SAT ∈ SPACE(n).

8. Prove { A | A is an NFA that rejects at least one string } ∈ NSPACE(n).

9. An important procedure in the proof of Savitch's Theorem is PathN(C1, C2, t, n) that decides existence of a path from C1 to C2 in the configuration graph of an NTM, N, with space complexity WN(n) ≤ d·f(n).
  1. Give precise specifications of the input parameters C1, C2, t, n, and the output from the procedure.
  2. Give pseudocode for the body of the procedure.
  3. Show that the space requirement for running PathN(C1, C2, t, n) is O(f(n)) × log2t.
  4. Show that the length of any computation branch of N ≤ αd·f(n)+1, where α = |Γ|+|Q|.
  5. Using the PathN procedure, give a DTM equivalent to N.
  6. Show that the space complexity function of the above DTM is O(f2(n)).
  7. Show how to overcome the above DTM's need to compute the value of f(n).
10. Prove NP ⊆ PSPACE = NPSPACE.

  2. Explain why the definition of PSPACE-complete problems uses polynomial-time reducibility rather than polynomial-space reducibility.

13. Describe DTMs and NTMs with a read-only input tape and how their space complexity functions are defined.

17. Describe log-space transducers.

18. Prove: Let M be any DTMR, NTMR, or LST using at most f(n) work-tape cells. Then WMtime(n) ≤ n αf(n)+1 = n 2O(f(n)), where α = |Γ|+|Q|.

  2. Define NL-complete problems.
20. Consider the proof of this theorem: If A ≤L B and B ∈ L, then A ∈ L. Let M be a log-space reduction transducer from A to B and MB a log-space DTMR deciding B.
  1. Explain why a sequential simulation of M and MB does not yield a log-space DTMR deciding A.
  2. Describe how to construct a log-space DTMR deciding A from modified M and MB.

Circuit Complexity and P-Completeness

25. Define each of the following:
  1. a circuit family
  2. what it means for a circuit family to decide a language over {0, 1}.
  3. equivalence of two circuits
  4. the size of a circuit
  5. size minimality of a circuit and a circuit family
  6. the size complexity of a circuit family
  7. the depth of a circuit
  8. depth minimality of a circuit and a circuit family
  9. the depth complexity of a circuit family
  10. the size and depth complexities of a language over {0, 1}
26.
  1. Give a proof idea for Theorem: If A ⊆ {0, 1}* is decided by a DTM with worst-case time complexity O(t(n)), then A has the circuit size complexity O(t2(n)).
  2. Prove that if A ∈ P, then A has a polynomial circuit size complexity.
  3. Prove that if A does not have a polynomial circuit size complexity, then A ∉ P.

  3. Define the processor and parallel time complexities of a circuit.

  5. Describe the evidence suggesting that P-complete problems are inherently sequential.
