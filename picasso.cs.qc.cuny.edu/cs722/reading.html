Required Reading

Introduction to the Theory of Computation, 3rd edition, Michael Sipser, Cengage Learning, 2013
ISBN-13: 978-1-133-18779-0
ISBN-10: 1-133-18779-X

The 2nd edition may be used as a substitute for the 3rd edition.
  • Self-study review of basic CS 220 and CS 320 materials:
    • Strings and Languages, pp. 13–14
    • Boolean Logic, pp. 14–15
    • Section 0.3: Definitions, Theorems, and Proofs
    • Section 0.4: Types of Proof
    • The Regular Operations, pp. 44–47
    • Nondeterminism, pp. 47–52
  • All of Chapter 3. You may skip Example 3.9, 3.11, 3.12.
  • Chapter 7: 7.1, 7.2, 7.3. You may skip Theorem 7.15 and 7.16.
  • Chapter 5: 5.3, pages 234, 235, 236, up to and including Corollary 5.23. (In 2nd edition, pages 206, 207, 208.)
  • Chapter 7: 7.4
The above is the reading assignment for the midterm exam.

The following is the reading assignment for the final exam.
  • Chapter 7: 7.5
  • Chapter 8: Everything from Definition 8.1 up to and including 8.5
    In 8.3, you may skip the proof of Theorem 8.9 (but read the proof idea) and Generalized Geography.
  • Chapter 10: 10.1
  • Chapter 9: 9.3 (You may skip the proofs of Theorem 9.30, 9.33, 9.34, but read the proof ideas.)
  • Chapter 10: 10.5: pages 427, 428 (pages 399, 400 in 2nd edition), the definition of the class NC on page 430 (page 402 in 2nd edition), Theorem 10.41, and P-Completeness on page 432 (page 404 in 2nd edition)
